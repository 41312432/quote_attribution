David (10 out of 10 )
The Incredibles was Pixar's longest film ever, and also had a very large number of sets. The writer/director, Brad Bird, was from "outside" - not a Pixar insider. He pushed the Pixar team to an even higher plane. The resulting film is a classic. Certainly the animation is beyond the merely excellent or fantastic. Watch the hair flying in the breeze, or the shadows on people's faces, or the fire and the water. What makes this movie one of the best animated films ever isn't the animation at all - it's the STORY. The Parrs are introduced to us, and we see ourselves in them. We sympathize with Helen Parr when she's trying to separate the fighting siblings, and we identify with Bob when the boss tells him that he's to look out for THE COMPANY and not the customer. Thus, having identified with the family, the later events in the family, especially to Helen Parr, become much more poignant and you might find yourself aching or weeping for these characters - despite the fact that they are not real at all. But then, they aren't any more real than someone like Dirty Harry or James Bond - but these characters grip you in your heart. Symbolism is rampant - watch carefully. What number is on Dash's jersey? How does Bob keep Syndrome from coming back to hurt his family? What is the word that Bob strains and struggles to say when he is about to go out and fight the baddie (after he gets out of the RV)? And what is Syndrome's final downfall? Hint: what does E say early on? This is not just one of Pixar's best - nor is it just one of the great triumphs in animation - this is nothing less than one of the best movies ever.
