Kevin (10 out of 10 )
I'm going to go one step further and point out that this screenplay is how a real screenplay ought to be written. To those aspiring screenwriters out there who use Final Draft like heroin, please nevermind or turn off the "CONTINUED:" and "(CONTINUED:)" sluglines in your scripts. That's taken care of once your script is purchased by the studio and is budgeted and retyped. Same goes for the scene numbers. These two blunders are really annoying for the producer who is reading your script after reading 43 others that day (hint, hint, HINT).

Kurt (10 out of 10 )
I think this is a work of genius. The dialogue is terrific and the depth of the charactors even better. It's one of the best movies I've seen. However, if this script is what got through the readers, it's a real eye opener just how many editing errors it has. If an unknown had submitted it, it would have gone to the round file. For example, the minor character 'Carl' is referenced multiple times where it is clear that 'Carol' was meant. There are many other accidental wordings. Don't get me wrong, I'm very happy it got past them. But again, it's an eye opener.

Kurt (9 out of 10 )
This is a terrific movie and script. In all the important ways, the script is one of the finest anywhere. However, it's an eye-opener that it had so many grammer, spelling and editing errors. A no-name writer probably would never get this past the first reading. Example: The minor charactor Carl is suddenly in the car with Simon and Melvyn saying a line that was obviously intended for Carol. The reason is probably a careless one-at-a-time global edit. Lots of other mistakes. Nevertheless, a great and well written movie.
