HARRY GEORGATOS (10 out of 10 )
This is easily another David Lynch twisted masterpiece that boggles the mind. It is a film that requires multiple viewings to really grasp any level of understanding when it comes to making it in the cutthroat business of Hollywood. Even though I enjoyed De Palma's Femme Fatale, Lynch's film is a far more sophisticated exercise when we delve into alternative realities. The alternate reality as portrayed in Mullholland Drive gives us the flip side of the Hollywood deam as experienced by Noami Watts. It gives such a ruthless insight into the politics of Hollywood, and the backstabbing, duplicitous nature of people working and trying to make it in the business. For me David Lynch has not yet made a bad or indifferent film. He is truly one of the great individual filmmakers left on the world stage, making his own personal nightmarish visions on film. David Lynch's Mulholland Drive along with Lost Highway will be films celebrated in fifty years from now as truly two of the great films of America.

Daniel Jones (10 out of 10 )
I never truly understood this movie until I viewed it under the influence of psychedelic mushrooms. I can't even begin to explain what I experienced. I hope to gain even more insight by reading the script. One of the best films ever made.

peter (8 out of 10 )
Being Australian, I was very surprised to see, apart from Naomi Watts, several other Aussie actors in this film. The film is very good in its presentation and needs to be watched closely. Typical of David Lynch to unleash such a grand film. It makes its mark on many levels and I think it gives an insight to what many aspiring actresses face on the road to stardom. With many twists and sub plots, including the famous masturbation scene by Naomi Watts, this is a great film.
