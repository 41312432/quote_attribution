What’s the point of Napoleon Dynamite? Well, it’s this: flying solo, that is, living life the stereotypically American way, the life of individualism, ends in misery. The only way one grows as a human, or even becomes human, is through a thick community of support, responsibility, and love. There are three key lines that reveal the meaning of the movie—and none of them are the seventy-four others that everyone quotes. The first is when Napoleon says, “Pedro, just listen to your heart. That’s what I do.” But here’s the problem: Napoleon does whatever his heart tells him, yet he’s still incredibly miserable. And the reason is this: what does it matter to follow one’s own heart when one’s heart is small or, to use a favorite word of Napoleon’s, decroded? The second critical line is Pedro’s: “If you vote for me, all of your wildest dreams will come true.” What the moviemakers seem to be saying through Pedro is this: if this kid can become class President, then really anyone’s wildest dreams can come true. But here’s the rub: Pedro’s dreams cannot come true without the support of the community. He, and through him, the filmmakers, explicitly call on the community to support him and, indirectly, to support one another. Lastly, a third important line is Kip’s: “Lafawnduh is the best thing that’s ever happened to me. I’m a hundred percent positive she’s my soul mate.” The point is obvious: any real human lover for Kip is better for his soul—makes for a better soul mate—than the relationship he had with, well, his technology.
