Marina (7 out of 10 )
Obviously the movie is fantastic. But this is not the script. This is an attempt to recreate the script by people who show an ignorance to the actual formatting. Action description is in the same margins as the dialogue AND encased in brackets. Parenthetical direction is also in brackets and with improper margins. Some direction is in the progressive present. Basically you can get a feel for the film, but if you're looking for examples of scripts do not pay any attention to this. The one for the fellowship however is in proper formatting and looks genuine. It is however (like most of them here), is in production stage formatting. Which isn't a hindrance in the least but if you're looking for examples while writing scripts it's important to note that in spec scripts there is no holding, no scene numbers and no camera directions.
