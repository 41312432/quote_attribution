Steven (10 out of 10 )
And we are about to get Biblical. I'm happiest when I'm griping about something I don't like about a screenplay, but in this case, I'm dry. This is simply a fantastic piece of writing in every respect. Brian Helgeland's characterization is virtuoso and the relationships he builds (most notably of course being Creasy and Pinta) progress so naturally and beautifully it almost seems as if they're not even being "written"; they're just so alive. I don't think there's a single line of dialogue that doesn't ring true. I've read this screenplay probably nine or ten times now and my printed copy is so bookmarked, I've started to lose track of my own notes. Especially noteworthy: Creasy's introduction; watch how little time it takes for you to get a nearly complete feel for him - beautiful descriptive economy. Also, the scene setup preceding Pinta's kidnapping: Helgeland started laying the pipe for this while the credits were still rolling. When it happens to Pinta, it's positively terrifying. Oh, Si Venda, if Scott's shooting/editing annoyed you in this movie, don't even touch Domino. It'll trigger an epileptic fit. Aside from that, Domino was just a terrible flick. Two hours you'll never get back.
