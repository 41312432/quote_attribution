debopratim (10 out of 10 )
This is the best mob or gangster movie ever made. It is useless to see the movie first without reading the novel. The idea of godfather being a magnum opus stems from the book and lends coherence to the script, which is naturally much more compact and less inclusive. The movie has got nothing to do with values, morals or love, as all the main characters violate every moral code in this movie, including promiscuity, betrayal, murder, treachery; lying being the least of it. The movie is about how apparently upright, caring and honest people can indulge in vices like murder without showing any remorse whatsoever, and later on start enjoying the very thing that they once hated. Makes it pretty hard to assign complete goodness or evil to any character in particular. The action scenes are piece of art, and unparalleled even in today's movies in terms of dramatization and execution. These scenes have been copied in thousands of movies in hundreds of languages. Right from the beheading of Khartoum to the strangulation of rizzi, every scene is grotesquely artistic in its depiction; masterclass. Coppolla taught them to do it, period. The script works because of Mario Puzo, whose involvement in the second part too managed to draw rave reviews. You are right Khara, this is not the final script, maybe the next draft was. I don't think it would be fair to judge the script and the movie without reading the book first; because even though you don't see them, these people are going through the events as depicted in the book.
