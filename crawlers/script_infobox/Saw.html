Drew (8 out of 10 )
Considering the movie was shot on a budget of 1 million dollars, it really looks and sounds quite good; the plot and writing is also quite good. I drop it two points because of the numerous plot holes. Most of them are fixed by revisionist history on the part of Saws II and III, but it's obvious that that's the writer/directors trying to retroactively cover them up rather than them being intended that way. (A few: Dr. Gordon could have easily gotten the phone at the end without needing to do what he does; the surveillance system Zep has at his disposal is dubious at best considering that he, presumably, just brought it with him; a chinsy microphone in a camera hidden behind two layers of glass probably could not pick up the sound of the tape recorder from the opposite end of the bathroom so Zep would have no way of knowing what Jigsaw's rules were; I seriously doubt that a skinny hobbling weakling in the advanced stages of some sort of poison could ever get the better of a solidly-built, six-foot-tall, trained police detective.) To answer the question of "Is Dr. Gordon dead?" let's look at the facts. First: despite that pathetic attempt at a tourniquet, he is still bleeding profusely, and can barely walk. Second point: as we see from Saw II, the house is somewhat large and hard to find the exit to, and it's on the end of a row of several of them. Third point: if Dr. Gordon took the phone with him, it would be easy for Allie to call the phone and have the police trace his location; even if he didn't, they could still call the phone and Adam would be able to. Fourth point: if Dr. Gordon manages to get himself to a hospital and live, or if the police traced the phone as above, he would be able to tell the police where Adam was. Even if the police had found his dead body dropped from blood loss, they would have found Adam soon after by searching the adjacent empty buildings as standard procedure. Fifth point: Adam knows what Jigsaw looks like, and so if he was found, he would be able to ID him. Sixth point: as of Saw II, this didn't happen, no mention is ever made of Dr. Gordon by the police as one of the victims, and Adam presumably had no hope of rescue by the time he died. Seventh point: Jigsaw knew that Dr. Gordon was loose, and technically by not killing Adam by 6 he did not succeed, and Jigsaw is not the type of person to leave loose ends. Based on all of that, I would say that Dr. Gordon is meat; I Would even go so far to say that Adam's continued imprisonment in the bathroom proves Gordon's death. As to the cause, I'd presume that Jigsaw has no particular interest in duking it out with anyone in his state, and simply watched Gordon to make sure that he didn't get near the exit to the house, then disposed of the body.
