Robyn H (10 out of 10 )
I saw "Buffy the Vampire Slayer" in a theater during it's initial run. I was only mildly interested, prior to that, in the art and craft of making films and television. I found the film fairly amusing, but actually thought while walking out of the theater, "If only the writer had more of a sense of hmmm, I don't know, _continuity_. That could have been a great movie." Continuity? I don't talk like that. What was going on? Even though I sat through all of the credits, I missed Joss's name. That film got me much more interested in filmmaking, and I've now become something of an interested amateur on the edge of the Hollywood writers community, and have been fortunate enough to become acquaintances with several writers who have had significant success in film and television. I did notice when Buffy came to television, but my snobbery wouldn't let me watch it until the very end of season 4, when I caught an episode by accident. I saw "Restless" and I was hooked. The writer I had accused of not understanding continuity, turned out to be the maestro of story weaving. So thank you, Joss. The failings of the Buffy movie gave me the impetus to gain the knowledge needed to appreciate the beautiful work you have created since. The crunchy Kuzui candy crap covering allowed me to savor the warm, wild and wonderful Whedon whackiness inside. Joss saved me, a lot.
