It's rather easy to label this movie as too long - specifically the beginning. If shortened, however, this film would be far from the same. As 'slow' as the start may seem in comparison to the Vietnam sequences, it's an essential setup - an elaborate painting of the lives of our characters - their families, their values, their culture, their friendship - all the things that the war tears to bits and pieces. Without them, all the latter scenes wouldn't have half the impact and "The Deer Hunter" would just be another war movie.

Mark S. (2 out of 10 )
"The Deer Hunter" film is one of my all-time favorites, but the script posted here is hardly even a rough draft. It has none of the staggeringly complex allegorical elements of the final product, nor the heartfelt interplay of most of the characters. Are we to assume that Cimino did all of the re-working of this script, or is there a Derek Washburn later version of this that became the shooting script? I would argue that there is literally not a single scene in this version we have here that is in the final film. Not one! Someone worked some incredible magic in transforming this overly crude, unmoving mess of a first draft into the polished masterpiece that it eventually became. I would love to know more about what went into this transformation.
