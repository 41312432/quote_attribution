Chris (4 out of 10 )
What good that exists in this movie does so only because it inhabits the Star Wars franchise as a whole: lightsabers and lasers and John Williams music are always cool things. Otherwise, it is a clumsy chunk of writing, directing, and storytelling that doesn't even have the same feel and zest of the original Star Wars classic trilogy. Throughout the film, characters will blurt out things in order for George Lucas to connect the continuity dots, and each connection is achieved with all the grace of a flying brick. It is clear that, in the writing of this movie, so many great storytelling opportunities were lost in favor of goofy choices that end up wasting precious screen time, leaving the robust topics of the Clone Wars and the Anakin's fall almost completely untouched for the remaining two films to handle. This film starts the Prequel trilogy off with a stumble that ultimately proved to be unrecoverable.
